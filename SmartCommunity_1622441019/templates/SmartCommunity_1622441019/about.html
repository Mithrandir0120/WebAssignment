About

A local migrant community group in Brisbane has many members who work odd hours, lack proper English skills. Due to these issues, they often struggle to find and negotiate with service providers such as plumbers, electrician, car repairs, air conditioning, building work etc. to fix the service issues in their homes. To help their members the community group offers help to fix the members’ service issues by contacting the service provider on the members’ behalf, negotiate a better price, and allocate the job on preferred day(date) and time as reported by the member.

The community group has few dedicated volunteers who help their members by operating different times of the day/evening from the community building office. A member calls the office via phone and reports his/her service issue. Often they turn up at the office to report their request.

The volunteer gather the details from the member, and write down in a paper, and assigns this job to a volunteer, who is available during the day. He/she creates an entry in the spreadsheet for this request with status “Open” together with all the details he/she recorded in the paper.

The volunteer who creates a request allocation must allocate the request to the volunteer who helps the members living in a specific area such as south of Brisbane, north of Brisbane, east of Brisbane or west of Brisbane. Also, volunteer should not have many jobs assigned to him/her to avoid the slow response time.

The volunteer contacts minimum three service providers via phone and negotiate a best price. He/she writes down the detail of each negotiation to report to the member either by him/her or another volunteer who is working during the preferred contact day/time of the member. A job is always allocated to the service provider who has quoted the minimum price for the service. The volunteer contacts the service provider again if the member accepts the offer and informs the member’s address details together with contact phone and prefer day/time. He/she also changes the request status to “Close.”

This system based on the paper and spreadsheet was working ok when the community members’ numbers were less. However, now the system is broken with several issues identified.

Members have reported that they never know the status of the request they have made. They can go days, sometimes weeks, without communication from the volunteers. In the eyes of members, no communication means no one is working on their request. They feel often volunteer do not know how to contact them to ask further questions or communicate progress. They often provide feedback about a service provider’s poor service but they feel that it is not property recorded. Members also feel that times to contact the volunteer is less, inconvenient and often when they contact either the phone line is busy or no one responds.

The volunteers feel they are overloaded. The request information is kept in Excel spreadsheet. Originally one or two volunteer were working but now there are several volunteers working independently. Each volunteer need to look or update the spreadsheet with information regarding the request they are allocated. The increasing numbers of the volunteers accessing the same spreadsheet causes the problems, because only one person can open and close the spreadsheet at any given time. The volunteers are also tired of constantly being called by the members wanting an update on the status of their request. Often there are few volunteers on certain days/times as often some volunteers do not turn up due to their personal problems during their time of service. There is also not adequate mechanism to record the member’s feedback about a service provider service.

Obviously the current system is not working well any more as neither the members nor the volunteers are happy with it. It is your job to come up with a system that addresses the needs of members and the volunteers as mentioned above.
